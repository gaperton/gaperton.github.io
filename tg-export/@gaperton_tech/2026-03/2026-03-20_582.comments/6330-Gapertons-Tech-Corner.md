Blower coolers are generally less sensitive (or sometimes even benefit) from vertical mounting compared to axial/open-air designs.Blowers suck air in from the front (usually the side facing into the case) and forcefully exhaust it straight out the back I/O bracket, directly outside the case.
 
Their heatsink fins are typically oriented horizontally (along the length of the card), and the cooling relies more on forced airflow through a ducted/shrouded path rather than relying on natural convection or large surface area exposed to case air.
 
Gravity has minimal impact on their performance because there's no large vapor chamber or long heatpipes that depend heavily on capillary action against gravity for liquid return. Many blowers use simpler heatpipe + fin designs, and the fan's high static pressure pushes air effectively regardless of orientation.
 
In vertical mounts, blowers often maintain similar temperatures to horizontal (or even run slightly cooler in some cases due to heat rising naturally toward the exhaust). They avoid the "suffocation" issue that axial coolers face when the fans are too close to glass panels — blowers don't intake from the side in the same way.

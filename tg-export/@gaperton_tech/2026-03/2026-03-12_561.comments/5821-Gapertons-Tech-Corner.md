__This document describes the core protocol for our team’s standup meetings. Different teams use different approaches; this is ours. These rules are enforced in every meeting, but we list them explicitly here as a shared reference.__
 
**Memo: Standup Rules**
 
Standup is a synchronization mechanism to establish the shared understanding of team intent. It is not a status ritual, a storytelling round, a mini design meeting. Participants are expected to adhere to the following rules:

• Always mention the ticket number when talking about your work; if there is no ticket, the moderator will create one as you speak.

• Do not jump into details about what you are doing right away. Always start with why and focus on what and what for.

• Do not assume everyone reads email or messenger. Mention important causes and outcomes aloud even if they were discussed offline.

• Do not assume you correctly guessed what people mean when something is unclear. Ask questions to validate your understanding before you allow yourself to agree or disagree.

• If you know you do not understand something, you are obliged to ask.
Never ignore or dismiss questions. “I don’t understand” is a polite way to say “your explanation is unclear” or “I think you are wrong.”

• If you believe someone is wrong, you may say “I don’t understand” and should ask question.

• When investigating, do not direct your questions to specific people assuming you know who has the answer or who should answer. Directing questions is reserved for the moderator.

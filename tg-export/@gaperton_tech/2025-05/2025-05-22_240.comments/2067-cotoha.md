If 𝐼 means "I want that", then:

𝐼 𝑎 = "I want that a"
¬ 𝐼¬ 𝑎 = "I do not want that not a" → i.e., "I want that a"
So, ¬𝐼¬𝑎 ≡ 𝐼𝑎

Now, the expression:

𝐼𝑎⇒¬𝐼¬𝑎
can be read as:

"If I want that a, then I do not want that not a."

📌 In plain English:
"If I truly want something to happen, then I can’t also want it not to happen."
This expresses the idea of consistency in desires — you can’t simultaneously want something and want its opposite.

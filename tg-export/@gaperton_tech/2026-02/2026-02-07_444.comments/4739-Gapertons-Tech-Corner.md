**Human-Gated Fixed-Homeostasis Agent**

The agent is a service agent acting on behalf of its owner. It exists to support the owner’s goals and interests; homeostasis is maintained solely as a means of fulfilling this role, not as an end in itself. 

Homeostasis is a set of normative preferences and invariants that define what matters to the agent and what counts as a normal or preferred state of affairs within the scope of its service role. The definition of homeostasis is not modifiable by the agent. 

Action constraints define fixed limits on admissible actions, independent of goals or homeostasis, and are not modifiable by the agent. 

The agent’s sole operational purpose is to maintain homeostasis within these constraints. It generates desires and candidate intents as instrumental means. The agent may observe and reason autonomously; any intent that would alter the state of the managed system or the external world requires a human to explicitly authorize execution.
